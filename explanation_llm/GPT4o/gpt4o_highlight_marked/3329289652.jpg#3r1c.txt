- **Legos** and **chess** are different activities and cannot be performed simultaneously.
- Playing with **Legos** involves building objects, while **chess** is a strategic board game.
- **Legos** are physical toys, whereas **chess** requires a chessboard and pieces.
- The act of **playing** with **Legos** does not overlap with the act of **playing** chess.
- The objects involved (Legos versus chess pieces) are distinct and used for different purposes.
