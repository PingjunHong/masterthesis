- Reading and watching are distinct activities; reading involves engaging with text, while watching involves viewing a visual medium.
- Books and shows are different formats; books are written works, while shows are visual productions typically aired on television or online platforms.
- It is not possible to watch a book; one can only read books and watch shows.
- Reading superhero books does not imply watching a superhero show; they are separate activities.
- Superhero books and superhero shows are not interchangeable; each offers a different experience and medium of storytelling.
- The act of reading does not involve viewing moving images as watching does.
