Dogs cannot be running and sleeping at the same time.
Running is an active state, while sleeping is a passive state.
Dogs need to be stationary to sleep, not moving or running.
Running near leaves implies movement, which contradicts the stillness required for sleeping.
Sleeping dogs typically do not engage in physical activity like running.
Being near leaves while running does not imply being on a pile of leaves while sleeping.
The physical activity of running contradicts the inactivity of sleeping.
Dogs are awake when running, but asleep when sleeping.
