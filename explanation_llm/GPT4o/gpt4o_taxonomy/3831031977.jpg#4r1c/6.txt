1. Looking down a mountain range is not the same as looking into the sky.
2. The direction of gaze cannot simultaneously be downward at a mountain range and upward at the sky.
3. The action of looking down a mountain range structurally conflicts with the action of looking into the sky.
4. The physical orientation required to look down a mountain range is incompatible with the orientation needed to look into the sky.
5. One cannot focus on a mountain range below and the sky above at the same time.
6. The logical structure of looking at a mountain range excludes the possibility of concurrently looking into the sky.
