1. Men cannot both be sitting on the bus and driving it simultaneously.
2. The action of sitting and the action of driving are mutually exclusive.
3. Men sitting on the bus implies they are passengers, not drivers.
4. If men are sitting, they are not in the driver's seat controlling the bus.
5. The location of the men being on the bus conflicts with the role of driving it.
6. Being seated on a bus precludes the ability to steer or operate it.
7. The structural roles of "sitting" and "driving" are logically exclusive in this context.
8. A person cannot occupy a passenger seat and perform the duties of a driver at the same time.
