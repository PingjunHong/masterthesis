- Jumping and sliding are different actions that cannot occur simultaneously.
- A rope does not function as a slide.
- Sliding requires a solid, smooth surface, unlike a rope.
- A crevasse is typically too narrow for sliding.
- Jumping implies an aerial movement, while sliding involves continuous contact with a surface.
- A slide is an object, whereas a rope is a tool used to assist in climbing or jumping.
- Slides are typically designed for playgrounds, not for crossing crevasses.
- Sliding over a crevasse would require a structure that doesn't exist in the premise.
- Rope facilitates jumping but does not provide a path for sliding.
- Mountain crevasses are natural formations, not equipped with slides.
