- If the woman is handing papers to the children, she cannot be keeping the paper to herself simultaneously.
- The action of handing over implies transfer to others, contradicting the notion of keeping something to oneself.
- To keep the paper to herself, the woman would not engage in the act of handing them to others.
- The semantics of "handing papers" involves giving, which directly opposes the idea of retaining possession.
- When papers are being distributed to others, they are no longer being kept by the original holder.
