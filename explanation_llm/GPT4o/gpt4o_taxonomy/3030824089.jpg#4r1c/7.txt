- A dog cannot be running and walking simultaneously.
- Running and walking are distinct actions.
- Running involves faster movement than walking.
- Walking implies a slower pace than running.
- A dog transitioning from running to walking would not be described as running.
- Running usually implies more exertion than walking.
- Walking is characterized by a steady, measured pace, unlike running.
- Running is typically more dynamic than walking.
- Walking implies a state of continuous foot contact, unlike running.
- Running involves both feet leaving the ground, which does not happen in walking.
