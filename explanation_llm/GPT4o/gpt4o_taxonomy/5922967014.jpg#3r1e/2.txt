- "At the beach" and "in the beach" both refer to being located at the beach, thus they convey the same meaning.
- "Three young boys" implies "boys" since it specifies the number but not the fact they are boys.
- The phrase "enjoying a day at the beach" implies that the boys are currently located at the beach.
- "Are in the beach" is semantically equivalent to "at the beach" in the context of being present at that location.
