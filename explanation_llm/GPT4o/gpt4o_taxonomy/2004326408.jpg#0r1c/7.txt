- Riding a bike is not the same as driving a car.
- You cannot ride a bike and drive a car simultaneously.
- Riding a bike involves pedaling, whereas driving a car involves operating a motor vehicle.
- A person on a bike is not inside a car.
- A shoulder bag is typically carried by a cyclist, not a driver.
- Bike shorts and a skirt are typical cycling attire, not typically worn while driving a car.
- Riding involves two wheels and manual power, while driving involves four wheels and engine power.
- The action of riding a bike is distinct from the action of driving a car.
- A woman riding a bike is visibly different from a woman driving a car.
